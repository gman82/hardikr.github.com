The last few months have been really, really crazy. Just look around and you can see disruption everywhere! Firstly, there were news that a viral Facebook app called threewords.me is about to be acquired just after a couple of days of launch.

Cheezburger network just raised 30 million dollars. 

WTF DO I DOEZ WIF 30 MILLION DOLLARS?

Something's not quite right. The Silicon Valley has gone crazy. I can accept that a Tumblr clone somehow managed to get accepted into YC , but seriously, Cheezburger network raising 30 million dollars?

It's not just the Valley, but here in the East too. 

  • Chinese mothers claim they're superior at what they do (sure, if what you do is, never mind... ).
  • Pakistan is under some sort of revolution.
  • Tunisia has already had it's revolution. And to think of it, one suicide sparked this movement. In India, even 17000+ farmers dying a year does nothing to provoke the masses. Yet, we think of ourselves to be the most pleasant community in the world.
  • Ditto with Egypt
  • India's per-capita income is now over $1000 (pdf). However, 80% of the population lives under $2 per day.
  • Here in India, the onion has become one of the most prized commodities that a middle class man can be proud to own. Coming home Mr. Kumar? You can be sure to have onions in every dish, coz we ain't no poor middle-class people.
  • The fuel price hikes are bringing out the worst in motorists.
  • The IN Congress, earlier the hallmark of democracy in India, has become a play-doll, doing nothing but holding meetings and deciding which ministers to keep on the pay-roll.

However, in India, some things are still the same, and it's strangely relieving (though highly frustrating).

  • Politicians are still siphoning off our money. Damn you fake democracy!
  • India still considers itself worthy of hosting a FIFA World Cup, after what happened in the CWG (ugh!).
  • Religious people are still dying in stampedes. It's frikkin 2011 people!! There's something known as management! And common sense!
  • Rahul Gandhi still doesn't have any achievements (apart from thinking he's young at 40).
  • People who cannot walk, but yet find time to do stuff, are still running states.
  • Shah Rukh is still trying his luck at game-shows (which is also 'inspired' by an American TV show. Seriously, can't Indians come up with anything original? )
  • Indian institutes of higher education are still deemed as elite and premier institutions.

What in God's name is going on? However, I must admit, I love civic disorder! People doubting their own governments? That's what I'm talking about!
